<p><p>A Coulomb electrostatic chuck is a device used in semiconductor manufacturing processes to hold and secure wafers or substrates during various stages of production. It utilizes the principle of electrostatic attraction to generate a strong hold on the substrate without the need for physical clamps or mechanical means. The chuck consists of a flat electrode with an insulating layer on top, creating an electric field that exerts a Coulomb force on the substrate.</p><p>The current outlook of the Coulomb electrostatic chuck market is quite promising. With the increasing demand for semiconductors in various industries such as electronics, automotive, and aerospace, the need for efficient and reliable production processes has also grown.
