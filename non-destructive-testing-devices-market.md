<p><p>Non-destructive testing devices refer to a variety of methods used to examine and inspect materials or products without causing damage. X-ray, ultrasonic, magnetic particle, and penetrant flaw detection are common types of NDT devices. X-ray testing utilizes radiation to identify defects in objects, while ultrasonic testing employs sound waves for flaw detection. Magnetic particle testing utilizes magnetic fields to identify surface and near-surface flaws, and penetrant flaw detection involves applying liquid dye to reveal defects. Additionally, eddy current testing uses electromagnetic induction, and other NDT methods may also be utilized to assess material integrity and quality.</p></p>
