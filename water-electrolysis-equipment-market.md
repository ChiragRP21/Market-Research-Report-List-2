<p><p>Water Electrolysis Equipment refers to devices that use electricity to split water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen gases. Traditional Alkaline Electrolysers are the most commonly used type, operating with a potassium hydroxide electrolyte solution and nickel electrodes. They are cost-effective but have lower efficiency. PEM Electrolysers, on the other hand, use a solid polymer electrolyte membrane, resulting in higher efficiency and flexibility in response to variable power inputs. They are more expensive but offer advantages like rapid start-up/shutdown and suitability for small-scale applications. Both types play an important role in the production of hydrogen as a clean energy source.</p></p>
